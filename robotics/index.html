Projects

1

Two Wheeled Drive

From fundamental designs like Line Follower and Obstacle Avoider to advanced creations including Swarm Robots and Maze Solvers, explore a spectrum of path planning and navigation challenges.

2

Robotic Arm4 Degrees of Freedom

Build a Robotic Arm with 4 Degrees of Freedom by assembling 3D printed parts. It consists of multiple joints, each capable of rotation along different axes. Inverse kinematics enables the calculation of the joint angles required to position the arm. This allows the arm to move in a coordinated manner, reaching desired positions and orientations with precision. Understand inverse kinematics and program the Robotic Arm it to simulate gestures, pick up, and drop objects.

3

Automatic Trash Can

Craft a servo-based control valve mechanism to open/close a trash can. Integrate an ultrasonic sensor into the system to detect the presence of a person nearby. Program arduino to control the servo based on data collected by the sensor.

4

Automatic LampHome Automation

Integrate light sensors to measure ambient light levels in the room. The system can then automatically swith on/off an electric lamp or turn it on/off based on the detected light levels.

Learning Outcomes

1

Understand the Basics of Electronics

Learn about the fundamental electronic building blocks including resistors, capacitors, LEDs, transistors, and their function in circuits. Practice building simple circuits using breadboards and jumper wires.

2

Learn to Program Arduino

Understand the basic syntax and structure of Arduino programming. Learn to import built-in and third-party libraries to extend the functionality of Arduino projects. Master the techniques for reading inputs from sensors (analog or digital) and controlling outputs to actuators.

3

Build your own Robot

Build your first robot by assembling mechanical parts and interfacing them with sensors and actuators. Learn how to control these actuators using PWM signals or motor drivers to achieve desired motion and functionality. Understand how to process sensor data and incorporate it into the control system for accurate and responsive operation.

4

Practice on a 3D Printer

Follow along as our experts demonstrate how to use a CAD software to design function, practical 3D models. Get familiar with slicing software that converts 3D models into printable instructions (G-Code). Learn about 3D printer operation, filament loading, print bed leveling, and troubleshooting by practicing with a 3D printer.
